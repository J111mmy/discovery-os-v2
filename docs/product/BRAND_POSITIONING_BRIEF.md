# DiscOS Positioning Strategy

Working strategy for the DiscOS Vercel app. This is a positioning document only; it does not change product source code, migrations, or database skill configuration.

## Executive Read

DiscOS should not be framed as an AI notes app, a research repository, or a generic product-management workspace. Those markets are crowded, well-funded, and already fluent in "AI insights."

The sharper category is:

**Traceable discovery intelligence.**

DiscOS turns customer signal into evidence-backed knowledge, then turns that knowledge into artifacts teams can defend: research syntheses, PRDs, GTM packs, stakeholder briefs, battle cards, action lists, and decision narratives. The strategic center is not storage or summarization. It is the evidence chain.

The product's strongest belief should stay visible everywhere:

**Traceability is the product.**

## Product Truth

Based on `CLAUDE.md`, the PRD, and the current app surface, DiscOS has a more opinionated architecture than most "AI for research" tools:

- Raw material becomes a `Source`.
- Relevant excerpts become `Source Segments`.
- Only selected, citable claims become `Evidence`.
- Agents classify, grade, synthesize, verify, detect gaps, and draft outputs from that evidence layer.
- The UI exists to expose, compose, and verify that intelligence for product, research, and GTM work.

This matters because it creates a different promise from "ask your data." DiscOS is closer to a discovery evidence graph that can produce defensible outputs.

## Market Landscape

### Research Repositories

Tools like Dovetail and Condens anchor around centralizing research, making findings searchable, and helping teams share insights. Dovetail already claims that AI-generated themes and insights trace back to source evidence, and its docs describe AI answers traced back to source material. Condens similarly positions around a centralized research repository, AI search, transcripts, cross-project analysis, and shared findings.

Implication: DiscOS cannot win by saying "research repository with AI." That is already an established lane.

### Customer Intelligence Platforms

Enterpret is the clearest modern signal in this market. It positions itself as customer intelligence infrastructure that structures support, sales, research, community, survey, CRM, and product signals into a continuously evolving customer understanding. It also emphasizes business outcomes such as churn, retention, adoption, support burden, and revenue.

Implication: "customer intelligence" is attractive but broad. DiscOS can borrow the ambition, but should not compete head-on with omnichannel VoC infrastructure until the product has broad integrations, outcome measurement, and enterprise workflows.

### Product Discovery And Prioritization

Productboard and Jira Product Discovery own much of the mental model around insight-to-feature and prioritization. Productboard describes itself as a customer-centric product management platform; Jira Product Discovery owns idea prioritization, roadmap alignment, and the Atlassian workflow.

Implication: DiscOS should integrate with the roadmap/prioritization world rather than look like a replacement on day one. The wedge is upstream: make the evidence behind decisions stronger before ideas become roadmap items.

### User Research And Testing Platforms

Maze and UserTesting focus on recruiting, testing, research execution, AI synthesis, and human insight. They own the "run research and get insight" workflow more than the "turn all existing evidence into traceable organizational intelligence" workflow.

Implication: DiscOS should not lead with "conduct research." It should lead with what happens after, around, and across research: compounding evidence, cross-project memory, citable outputs, and decision confidence.

### Naming Collision Risk

DISCO is a live legal/eDiscovery software brand. That matters because DiscOS sits near evidence, documents, AI, traceability, and discovery language. Even if the spelling differs, the market adjacency creates search, pronunciation, and confusion risk.

## Core Positioning

For product, research, and GTM teams doing serious customer discovery, DiscOS is a traceable discovery intelligence platform that turns scattered customer signal into evidence-backed knowledge and defensible outputs.

Unlike AI summarizers or research repositories, DiscOS preserves the full chain from raw source to citable evidence to finished artifact, so teams can move faster without losing confidence in what the evidence actually supports.

## Narrative

Teams have more customer signal than ever: interviews, calls, support tickets, research notes, CRM updates, sales objections, docs, and Slack threads. But the signal does not compound. It gets summarized, scattered, rewritten, and eventually detached from the evidence that made it credible.

AI can make this worse. Faster unsupported summaries create the appearance of insight without the confidence to act.

DiscOS starts from a different premise: every useful output should be traceable back to exact evidence.

It separates raw sources from source segments and evidence. Agents then extract citable claims, connect people and companies, synthesize themes, detect gaps, verify claims, and draft artifacts. Teams get speed, but every recommendation and proof point can still travel back to source.

DiscOS is not another place to store research. It is the evidence intelligence layer that turns discovery into decisions, artifacts, and organizational memory.

## Message Pillars

### 1. Trace Every Claim

Every recommendation, customer observation, sales proof point, and product decision can link back to the exact evidence behind it.

Proof to show:

- Inline citations in generated artifacts
- Source sentence, speaker, timestamp, project, and classification
- Claim verification status
- Confidence labels and evidence grading

### 2. Turn Signal Into Compounding Knowledge

Every interview, call, ticket, and document strengthens a living knowledge base instead of disappearing into folders, private memory, or one-off summaries.

Proof to show:

- Cross-project evidence
- People, company, and competitor digests
- Theme and problem synthesis
- Adjacent-project signal detection
- Research freshness and confidence scoring

### 3. Ship Outputs People Can Defend

Agents draft the artifact, but the evidence graph keeps it specific, grounded, and reviewable.

Proof to show:

- Session briefs
- PRDs
- GTM packs
- Stakeholder decks
- Sales battle cards
- Action extraction
- Citation-backed recommendations

## ICP And Wedge

Initial ICP:

- B2B product teams running recurring customer discovery
- PMs who must turn messy evidence into decisions and artifacts
- Researchers who need their work to influence product and GTM
- Founders or product leaders selling complex products and needing evidence-backed positioning
- GTM teams who need customer proof without inventing unsupported claims

Best early wedge:

**High-stakes B2B discovery where the cost of unsupported claims is visible.**

Examples:

- Building a PRD from multiple customer interviews
- Turning discovery calls into a stakeholder decision brief
- Producing GTM material from validated customer evidence
- Tracking why prospects choose or reject competitors
- Showing leadership exactly what evidence supports a roadmap bet

Expansion path:

1. Product managers and researchers
2. Product leadership and founders
3. GTM teams using evidence for sales and marketing
4. Customer success and support teams feeding signal into the evidence graph

## Competitive Position

DiscOS should position against alternatives by reframing the job:

- Not "store research" but "make customer evidence usable in decisions."
- Not "summarize calls" but "produce citable knowledge."
- Not "prioritize features" but "prove what should be prioritized."
- Not "ask your data" but "verify what the data supports."
- Not "generate artifacts" but "generate artifacts that can defend themselves."

## Risks

1. **Traceability is not unique enough by itself.** Dovetail already uses traceability language. DiscOS must show a stronger chain from evidence to artifact to decision, not only citations.
2. **The category may be too abstract.** "Evidence intelligence" can sound vague. Use concrete proof moments in demos and copy.
3. **DiscOS name may create confusion.** DISCO in legal/eDiscovery is close enough to matter. See the naming exploration.
4. **Generic AI expectations can commoditize the product.** Buyers may compare it to ChatGPT, NotebookLM, Notion AI, or Glean unless the evidence workflow is made obvious.
5. **Repository gravity is dangerous.** If the app looks like a storage tool, it will be compared to mature repository products.
6. **Security and privacy must be table stakes.** Source material includes confidential interviews, customer data, and competitive intelligence.
7. **Too many personas can blur the wedge.** PMs should lead the first story; GTM is the expansion story.

## Opportunities

1. **Own citable outputs.** The market talks about insights; DiscOS can own artifacts that carry their evidence with them.
2. **Make confidence visible.** Confidence, source diversity, freshness, and claim verification can be a distinctive product language.
3. **Use the evidence chain as the demo.** The "aha" should be clicking a claim and walking backward to source, then forward into PRD/GTM/deck output.
4. **Turn research into revenue language.** GTM teams need customer proof, objections, competitive intelligence, and differentiated claims.
5. **Build a bridge between research and roadmap tools.** DiscOS can feed evidence into Productboard, Jira Product Discovery, Linear, and decks rather than replace them immediately.
6. **Make every paid session feel durable.** If pricing uses sessions, the value story should be that each session creates reusable intelligence, not disposable AI output.

## Sales Talk Track

Most teams already have the raw customer signal. The problem is that the signal does not compound. It gets trapped in transcripts, folders, support tools, call notes, and individual memory. Then roadmap discussions and GTM claims become debates about interpretation.

DiscOS gives you a traceable intelligence layer. It separates raw sources, source segments, and evidence, so every generated output can cite the exact material behind it. Agents do the synthesis and drafting work, but the team never loses the trail.

That means faster research synthesis, more defensible product decisions, stronger GTM proof, and less time recreating context.

## Brand Voice

DiscOS should sound rigorous, useful, and commercially literate. It should feel like a senior research analyst, product strategist, and evidence librarian working together.

Voice rules:

- Prefer exactness over hype.
- Show the evidence chain whenever possible.
- Talk about speed through confidence, not magic.
- Treat unsupported claims as risk.
- Make product, research, and GTM feel like one evidence loop.

Words to own:

- traceable intelligence
- evidence-backed
- exact evidence
- citable claims
- source-to-output
- confidence
- defensible decisions
- discovery memory
- evidence chain
- organizational intelligence

Words to avoid:

- AI copilot
- magic
- instant insights
- all-in-one workspace
- meeting notes app
- research graveyard
- single source of truth, unless paired with exact citation behavior

### Live framing decisions (extend, do not contradict the brief)
- **Category expression in market = "One source of proof."** Sanctioned twist on "single source of truth," allowed ONLY because always paired with citation/proof behavior. Never use the bare phrase "single source of truth."

- **Lead with the room, show breadth as the expansion.** Deck shows six seats (research, design, product, sales, success, leadership) for the whole-org "one well" vision, but in delivery lead with the PM/research/design seat in the room. Honors the wedge rule: PMs lead, GTM expands, breadth is the vision not the wedge.

### Messaging hooks
- **"Garbage In, Gospel Out."** The AI-era mutation of GIGO: AI launders unverified input into confident, authoritative-looking output everyone treats as truth. DiscOS does NOT claim to fix bad input (no "garbage in, gold out" — that breaks the trust positioning and the "exactness over hype" voice rule). The honest, on-brand flip: DiscOS makes provenance visible so garbage can never masquerade as gospel. Lines: "You can't fix garbage in. We make sure it never comes out dressed as gospel." / "We don't promise gold out. We promise you always know which is which." Use as a "why now" opener (deck slide 4 / cold-email hook).
